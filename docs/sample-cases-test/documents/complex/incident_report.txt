ACME CORPORATION — INFORMATION SECURITY INCIDENT REPORT
Report ID: ACME-IR-2026-0312
Case Reference: CYB-2026-0045
Classification: CONFIDENTIAL — ATTORNEY-CLIENT PRIVILEGED (PENDING)
Prepared By: Marcus Chen, CISO
Date: March 14, 2026

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
On March 12, 2026, Acme Corporation detected unauthorized encryption activity across production
file servers and customer database replicas. Forensic analysis confirmed a LockBit 3.0 ransomware
variant. Attackers exfiltrated approximately 847 customer records before deploying encryption.
Operations were partially restored by March 14, 2026. Outside counsel (FMG) engaged same day.

INCIDENT TIMELINE
March 10, 2026  02:14 UTC  — Initial VPN authentication from IP 185.220.101.44 (Tor exit node)
March 10, 2026  02:31 UTC  — Lateral movement to ACME-FS-PROD-03 via compromised service account
March 11, 2026  11:47 UTC  — Bulk query against CRM database (customer_pii table)
March 12, 2026  06:02 UTC  — Ransom note deployed (readme-lockbit.txt) on 14 hosts
March 12, 2026  08:19 UTC  — SOC alert triggered; incident response team activated
March 12, 2026  14:00 UTC  — FMG notified; preservation hold issued on affected systems
March 13, 2026  09:30 UTC  — Decryption keys obtained via cyber insurance panel vendor
March 14, 2026  16:45 UTC  — Core CRM and billing systems restored from clean backups

AFFECTED SYSTEMS
- ACME-FS-PROD-03, ACME-FS-PROD-07 (file servers)
- ACME-CRM-DB-01 (PostgreSQL — customer records)
- ACME-BACKUP-02 (backup server — partial compromise)
- 847 endpoints with encrypted local caches (sales laptops)

DATA EXPOSURE — PII INVENTORY
Confirmed exfiltration of 847 customer records containing:
  - Full names, mailing addresses, email addresses
  - Last four digits of payment card numbers (tokenized vault breach)
  - Account numbers and subscription tier data
  - 23 records included Social Security numbers (legacy migration data)

No employee HR records confirmed exfiltrated at time of report.

IMMEDIATE ACTIONS TAKEN
1. Isolated affected VLAN segments; revoked all VPN sessions
2. Reset credentials for 412 privileged and service accounts
3. Engaged CrowdStrike IR retainer and FMG for regulatory guidance
4. Preserved firewall, VPN, and Active Directory logs for forensic review
5. Notified cyber insurance carrier (CyberGuard Policy CYB-ACME-2024-9912)

REGULATORY NOTIFICATION STATUS
- State AG notifications: Under review (CA, NY, TX thresholds triggered)
- FTC/HHS: Not applicable (no PHI confirmed)
- SEC disclosure: Pending board review (materiality assessment underway)

RISK ASSESSMENT
Overall Risk Level: CRITICAL
Business impact: $1.2M estimated downtime; reputational exposure significant.
Recommended next steps: Complete forensic report, customer notification plan, MFA rollout.
